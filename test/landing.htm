APC Projects in the Pipeline

Indonesia: A Collaborative Community Development Effort in Low-Cost Public Housing Community   

During our last roundtable session in Jakarta in August, our Indonesian members rallied up to offer their diverse resources for the Rusunawa Pinus Elok project. GK Indonesia will continue to be responsible for Community Building & Overall Coordination; Tanoto Foundation will also manage and operate an early childhood center/ kindergarten; the Tahir Foundation will look at preventative healthcare while government will construct and operate a healthcare clinic. Jababeka will help develop a playground for children, and Victor Hartono has offered trees and plants from the foundation nursery to “green” the project. This has proven to be a great example that with enough members in-country, the collective resources of our members can result in an impressive collective impact!
